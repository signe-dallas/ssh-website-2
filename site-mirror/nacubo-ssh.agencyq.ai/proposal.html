Core Solutions

6 Pain Points, Addressed

We mapped each pain point from the initial brief to specific features built in this prototype, complete with research evidence and measurable KPI targets.

1

Front-Matter Pages Are Too Text-Heavy

What We Heard

Campus leaders reported that dense, paragraph-heavy pages forced them to read extensively just to understand whether the Hub was useful. When value wasn't immediately clear, they disengaged.

How We Will Solve It

  • Card-based layouts on every page

    Scannable units that surface relevant content instantly.

  • Scannable stats with key numbers

    Key metrics visible at a glance without reading paragraphs.

  • Progressive disclosure via accordions

    Clean pages with optional depth for those who want it.

  • Inverted pyramid content structure

    Most important content firstâ€”core message visible above the fold.

  • Strategic whitespace and visual hierarchy

    Content has room to breathe, guiding eyes to key elements.

2

Experience Feels Static and Read-Only

What We Heard

Users described the Hub as a reference site, not a working platform. The lack of interactivity discouraged exploration and repeated use. They wanted to do something, not only read.

How We Will Solve It

  • QuickMatch 3-question quiz in hero

    Three questions deliver personalized toolkit recommendations in seconds.

  • Animated stats with count-up

    Animated numbers signal an active, engaging platform.

  • Hover animations on all cards

    Subtle hover effects signal interactivity and clickability.

  • Interstitial page with toolkit preview

    Pulls toolkit information from outside the app to set expectations before login, reducing drop-off.

  • FilterTabs for content browsing

    Instant content filtering by topic, format, or date.

Users Skip Front Matter to Find Toolkits

What We Heard

The most common behavior was skipping directly to toolkits. Users saw front-matter pages not as helpful context, but as barriers standing between them and the tools they came for.

How We Will Solve It

  • Hub-and-spoke navigation architecture

    One-click path to toolkits from any page.

  • Value-first content showcase

    Insights, videos, and toolkit previews displayed prominently upfront.

  • Contextual toolkit recommendations

    Front-matter pages suggest relevant toolkits based on content context.

  • Clear value propositions on every page

    Every front-matter page answers "Why should I read this?" in the first 5 seconds.

Critical Advantage

AI Discoverability Changes Everything in 2026

In 2025, Google AI Overviews cut organic click-through rates by 61%. Zero-click searches hit 69%. AI referral traffic surged 527%. The rules of visibility have fundamentally changed, and the current Student Success Hub site is invisible to every one of these systems.

The current site is completely invisible to AI

Every page on nacubostudentsuccess.org contains a noindex, nofollow meta tag that blocks all search engines and AI crawlers. There is no sitemap (returns 404), no structured data, no meta descriptions, and no Open Graph tags. All 22 case studies live as Dropbox PDFs. All 11 toolkits sit behind a login wall. When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google about student success resources in higher education, NACUBO does not appear.

The Four Moves That Make NACUBO Citable

Each of these transforms content that AI systems currently ignore into content they actively cite, recommend, and link to.

22 Use Cases: PDF to Web

Before

Dropbox-hosted PDFs with no summaries. AI crawlers skip them entirely.

After

22 dedicated pages, each with full narrative, results, institutional quotes, and Article schema.

When a campus leader asks ChatGPT "How are community colleges using data to improve student outcomes?", our Alamo Colleges and El Camino use cases surface as citable answers.

11 Toolkits: Locked to Public

Before

Names and descriptions locked behind a login wall. AI knows nothing about them.

After

Each toolkit has a public detail page with overview, value proposition, implementation levels, and LearningResource schema.

When Perplexity searches "What tools exist for equity-centered budgeting in higher ed?", our Equity-Centered Budgeting Toolkit appears with a direct link.

From Silent to Speakable

Before

No structured data. No sitemap. No meta descriptions. Empty robots.txt.

After

14 schema types, llms.txt, AI-specific bot permissions, 42-URL sitemap, SpeakableSpecification markup.

We built the site to be machine-readable from day one. AI systems don't guess what our pages contain. They know.

Events: Invisible to Rich

Before

9 webinars embedded as iframes with no titles, dates, descriptions, or transcripts.

After

Full EducationEvent schema with speakers, dates, topics, free attendance offers, and organized webinar series.

Google AI Overviews pull event data directly from EducationEvent schema. Our events appear in "upcoming webinars about student success" queries.

The Hub has exceptional content: 11 toolkits, 22 institutional case studies, expert webinars, and a Toolkit Finder assessment. None of it reaches AI systems today. Our prototype makes all of it discoverable, structured, and citable from the moment it goes live.

Technical Advantages

Beyond solving user pain points, the prototype runs on a modern technical foundation that scales and stays discoverable long-term.

Performance

Runs on Next.js 16 with Turbopack. Sub-2-second page loads, optimized images via next/image, and code-split routes for instant navigation.

SEO & Discoverability

Server-rendered pages with semantic HTML, structured metadata, Open Graph tags, and sitemap generation. Every page is indexable and shareable.

AI / AIO Readiness

Clean semantic markup and structured content that AI systems parse and reference. Ready for AI-generated overviews and citations.

Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)

Skip links, keyboard navigation, ARIA landmarks, focus indicators, reduced-motion support, and 4.5:1+ contrast ratios throughout.

Interactive Components

8+ interactive components including QuickMatch, Accordion, FilterTabs, Toolkit Interstitial Page, AnimatedStats, and more.

Cross-Linking Architecture

Hub-and-spoke information architecture connecting every page to toolkits. Breadcrumbs, contextual CTAs, and related content on every page.
